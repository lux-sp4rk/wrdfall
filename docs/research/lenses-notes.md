# The Art of Game Design ("Book of Lenses") — working notes for wordfall

These are **paraphrased, non-quotational** notes: a practical checklist to make wordfall fun (especially for a senior-first, calm puzzle).

## Why this matters
If the core interaction isn’t satisfying, monetization is irrelevant. Our job is to find the *smallest fun thing* and amplify it.

## A lightweight “lenses” checklist (use every build)
### 1) Lens of **Core Experience**
- What emotion are we aiming for in a 3–5 minute session? (cozy pride? quiet relief? cleverness?)
- What’s the one sentence promise the game keeps?

### 2) Lens of **Clarity**
- Does the player understand the goal in <30 seconds without coaching?
- Are rules visible at the moment they matter?

### 3) Lens of **Tactile Satisfaction** (wordfall-specific)
- Do drag/drop and “locking” a word feel *good* (sound, animation, snap)?
- Is the "submit" moment rewarding even for a small word?

### 4) Lens of **Meaningful Choice**
- Are there interesting decisions, or is there only one obvious move?
- If there’s a hint, does using it feel like a choice—not punishment?

### 5) Lens of **Challenge Curve**
- Is early success guaranteed? (especially for seniors)
- Do we have a gentle ramp: easy wins → small constraints → multi-slot puzzles?

### 6) Lens of **Feedback**
- When something is invalid, does the game explain *why* (which rule failed) in friendly language?
- Does the game celebrate progress (slot filled, tapestry complete) without being noisy?

### 7) Lens of **Flow / Friction**
- Where does the player hesitate?
- Any mis-taps, tiny buttons, or “UI tax” steps?

### 8) Lens of **Aesthetics & Theme**
- Does it feel calm/cozy (palette, typography, motion)?
- Are we avoiding harsh buzzer sounds / red error states?

### 9) Lens of **Replay / Retention**
- Do players want “one more”? Why?
- Can we offer variety via packs/themes without complexity?

### 10) Lens of **Audience Fit (Mom test)**
- What is confusing or stressful specifically for Giselle?
- Would she recommend it to a friend? What would she say it is?

## Playtest script (10 minutes)
1) Give them the link. Say only: “Try it.”
2) Observe silently for 60–90 seconds.
3) Ask:
   - What do you think you’re supposed to do?
   - What felt good?
   - What felt annoying/confusing?
   - Would you play again tomorrow? Why/why not?
4) End with: “If you could change one thing, what would it be?”

## wordfall “fun hypothesis” (draft)
- The fun is **weaving**: assembling a word feels like placing the last piece of a small puzzle.
- The satisfaction is **locking** it in and watching the tapestry fill.
- The calm comes from **no timers, no pressure**, and forgiving hints.

## Next step
After we have the first playable, run the checklist and do a Mom/Hector test *immediately*—don’t wait for polish.
