# wordfall Design Context

## Users

**Primary**: Word puzzle enthusiasts of all ages
- Broad demographic appeal with senior-first accessibility as foundation
- Players seeking intellectual engagement over fast-paced reflex challenges
- Cross-platform users: iPad, mobile, and browser
- Multilingual: English and Spanish speakers

**Context**: Playing during short breaks, commuting, or relaxing moments

**Job to be done**: Find satisfying word combinations in a calm, strategic environment

**Emotional goals**: 
- Relaxed focus — engaging but not stressful
- Intellectual satisfaction — the pleasure of finding clever word combinations
- Sense of accomplishment without urgency

---

## Brand Personality

**Three words**: Sharp, modern, thoughtful

**Voice & tone**: 
- Confident but not arrogant
- Clean and intentional, never cluttered
- Respects the player's intelligence
- Calm, like water flowing over stones

**Emotional goals**:
- Create a meditative state through gameplay
- Reward strategic thinking
- Feel premium without being pretentious

## Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone**: Minimal, modern word puzzle

**Theme foundation**: Waterfall-inspired blue palette (dark mode default)
- Deep slate blues as base (`#2B3D4F`)
- Flowing, water-like gradients and subtle motion
- Cascading visual metaphors (letters "falling" like water)

**Color strategy**:
- Keep the existing blue-dark theme as primary
- Add waterfall-inspired accents: subtle aqua highlights, flowing gradients
- Use light mode as secondary (cream/warm background for variety)
- Primary terracotta `#E07857` becomes a warm accent against cool blues

**Anti-references**:
- NOT candy-crush colorful (avoid saturated primaries, busy patterns)
- NOT corporate sterile (avoid flat grays, generic sans-serif coldness)

**References to capture**:
- Scrabble's tile tactility and letterforms
- Tetris's satisfying line-clear mechanics (but calmer pacing)
- Monument Valley's spatial minimalism and serene color palettes

---

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration** — Every element must earn its place. Remove visual noise, keep only what serves the player's focus.

2. **Water as metaphor** — Flowing lines, cascading animations, deep blues, and the sense of momentum-with-calm. Letters should feel like they're falling through water.

3. **Accessible without compromise** — Senior-first means large tap targets, high contrast, and clear typography. These improve the experience for everyone.

4. **Satisfaction through simplicity** — Clean grids, minimal UI chrome, rewarding micro-interactions. The pleasure of placing a perfect word should feel tactile.

5. **Hand-holding tutorials** — Guide new players explicitly. Show, don't just tell. Progressive disclosure: start simple, reveal depth gradually.

## Visual Language

**Color palette**:
- **Primary background** (dark): `#2B3D4F` — Deep slate waterfall pool
- **Card background** (dark): `#364A5E` — Lighter flowing water
- **Primary accent**: `#E07857` (terracotta) — Warm stone in cool water
- **Secondary accent**: `#7A9D8C` (sage) — Mossy stone, natural calm
- **Text** (dark mode): `#F2F2F2` primary, `#99BFB3` secondary
- **Light mode**: Keep existing warm cream `#F5F2E8`

**Typography**:
- **Font**: Inter (humanist, highly legible)
- **Scale**: Senior-friendly sizing (no body text below 16px)
- **Weights**: 400 for body, 600-700 for UI, bold for scores

**Shapes**:
- **Cards**: 24px radius (soft, welcoming)
- **Buttons**: 12-16px radius (friendly but precise)
- **Game tiles**: Sharp corners (tile-like, tactile)

**Shadows & depth**:
- Soft, diffused shadows (water doesn't cast hard shadows)
- 8px offset, 20px blur, low opacity (12-30%)
- Avoid harsh drop shadows

**Motion**:
- Smooth, liquid transitions (150-300ms ease)
- Reduced motion support required
- Gravity physics should feel weighty but fluid

---

## Accessibility Requirements

- **WCAG 2.1 AA minimum** for all text and UI
- **48px minimum touch targets** (already implemented)
- **High contrast mode** support (already implemented)
- **Reduced motion** support (already implemented)
- **Color blind safe**: Don't rely solely on color for game state (use icons, patterns)
- **Keyboard navigation** fully supported (focus states, logical tab order)
- **Screen reader** labels for all interactive elements

---

## Tutorial Philosophy

**Hand-holding approach**:
- Explicit instructions with visual highlighting
- Gated interactions — player must complete each step before proceeding
- Contextual tips that appear when needed
- Celebrate first achievements (first word, first 4-letter word, etc.)
- Optional replay for returning players

---

## What Makes wordfall Distinctive

- **Not frantic**: Calm pacing with strategic depth
- **Not childish**: Sophisticated aesthetic, intellectual challenge
- **Not sterile**: Warm accents, natural metaphors, humanist typography
- **Not overwhelming**: Clean UI, clear hierarchy, senior-first clarity
- **The waterfall**: Letters cascade, flow, and pool. The game feels like watching water find its path.
