## Design Context

### Users

**Primary Audience:** Word game enthusiasts — people who actively seek out and enjoy word puzzles like Scrabble, crosswords, and similar games.

**Context & Job to be Done:**
- Players seek intellectual satisfaction from finding clever words and strategic gameplay
- They want to feel accomplished when forming longer words, building combo streaks, and clearing the board
- Play sessions may vary from quick breaks to longer strategic sessions
- Cross-platform use: iPad at home, phone on commutes, browser when convenient
- Bilingual players (English and Spanish)

### Brand Personality

**Voice & Tone:** Encouraging and calm. The interface should feel like a warm invitation to think, not a stressful challenge.

**Three-Word Personality:**
1. **Calm** — Unhurried, zen-like experience with thoughtful pacing
2. **Thoughtful** — Rewarding strategic thinking and careful word selection
3. **Timeless** — Classic word game aesthetics that don't chase trends

**Emotional Goals:**
- Create a sense of accomplishment through clear feedback and score multipliers
- Evoke the satisfying "aha!" moment of discovering a clever word
- Build confidence with intuitive, predictable interactions
- Maintain a cozy, warm atmosphere that feels like a favorite board game

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual Tone:** Flat design with playful colors and friendly UI elements. Clean, minimal, and inviting.

**Key References:**
- **Two Dots** — Flat design, playful but sophisticated color palette, friendly UI, accessible spacing
- **NYT Games (Wordle)** — Clean typography, uncluttered layouts, focus on the core experience
- **Headspace / Calm** — Soothing color transitions, breathing room, senior-friendly clarity

**Anti-References (Explicitly Avoid):**
- Corporate/sterile aesthetics — avoid feeling generic, clinical, or soulless
- Cartoon/childish designs — maintain sophistication for adult word enthusiasts
- Neon/gaming aesthetics — not competitive esports; focus on calm contemplation
- Skeuomorphic textures — no faux wood, leather, or realistic buttons
- Cluttered/busy interfaces — keep visual noise minimal

**Color Palette:**
- **Primary:** Terracotta (#E07857) — warm, inviting, energetic without being aggressive
- **Secondary:** Sage (#7A9D8C) — calming, natural, supports focus
- **Light Mode:** Cream background (#F5F2E8), white cards, charcoal text
- **Dark Mode:** Dark teal (#2B3D4F), muted card backgrounds, high contrast text

**Typography:**
- **Primary Font:** Inter (variable weight) — highly readable, modern, excellent for UI
- **Display Sizes:** Large, legible headings with generous letter spacing
- **Body Text:** Clear hierarchy with strong contrast for readability

**Theme Support:**
- Both light and dark modes supported
- Theme switching persists across sessions
- Colors adapt while maintaining brand personality in both modes

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity Over Decoration** — Every visual element should serve a purpose. Remove anything that doesn't aid comprehension or delight.

2. **Generous Touch Targets** — Minimum 48×48px for all interactive elements. Senior-friendly design requires easy, accurate tapping.

3. **High Contrast Always** — Text must be readable in all lighting conditions. WCAG AA minimum, with AAA aspirations where possible.

4. **Calm Pacing** — Animations should feel smooth and unhurried. Avoid jarring transitions or aggressive motion.

5. **Reward Feedback** — Celebrate achievements clearly but elegantly. Score multipliers, combo streaks, and word formations deserve satisfying visual feedback.

6. **Bilingual Fluency** — Design must accommodate both English and Spanish seamlessly, including special characters (Ñ) and appropriate text expansion.

7. **Cross-Platform Consistency** — Visual language should translate beautifully across iPad, mobile, and browser while respecting each platform's conventions.

8. **Accessibility First** — Reduced motion support, screen reader compatibility, and keyboard navigation are not afterthoughts.
